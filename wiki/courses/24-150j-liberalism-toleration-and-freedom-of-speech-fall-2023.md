# Liberalism, Toleration, and Freedom of Speech

This course examines&nbsp;theories and principles that&nbsp;underlie the concept of free speech&nbsp;in the United States, the historical context in which the values of free speech and toleration emerged,&nbsp;and the&nbsp;philosophical arguments&nbsp;that were and are&nbsp;made for and against them. Students analyze a&nbsp;variety of&nbsp;contexts and communicative practices, including&nbsp;new media technologies,&nbsp;to&nbsp;debate how “speech” can be described and when it should be appropriately regulated.&nbsp;The course also considers current disputes over free speech on&nbsp;college campuses.
