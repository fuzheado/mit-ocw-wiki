# Stochastic Processes, Detection, and Estimation

This course&nbsp;examines the fundamentals of detection and estimation for signal processing, communications, and control. Topics covered include: vector spaces of random variables; Bayesian and Neyman-Pearson hypothesis testing; Bayesian and nonrandom parameter estimation; minimum-variance unbiased estimators and the Cramer-Rao bounds; representations for stochastic processes, shaping and whitening filters, and&nbsp;Karhunen-Loeve expansions; and&nbsp;detection and estimation from waveform observations. Advanced topics include: linear prediction and spectral estimation, and&nbsp;Wiener and Kalman filters.
